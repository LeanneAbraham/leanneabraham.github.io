About Me

World Regional Geography Reference Maps

These maps were produced to accompany an open source World Regional Geography textbook that will be released online in late 2018/early 2019. This project, and the resulting maps featured below, are the end product of a year of thinking about how to design a project with it’s handoff in mind. The design spec for this project is the culmination of a year of careful thought and intentionally created to result in maps that are open source, accessible, and easily reproducible.

Data

These maps use data from Natural Earth, a hillshade generated using a mosaiced 90m STRM DEM, and Jeremy Tribby’s font Barlow.

Process

I started by creating a reference global base map with the earth subdivided into the 10 regions featured in the text. Each region was given a unique colorway to be utilized in the design of the reference maps for that region.

After an appropriate projection was selected, a DEM was reprojected and exported as a tiff from ArcMap. All terrain was generated using Daniel Huffman’s brilliant Blender tutorial. Simultaneously, an Ai file was exported which contained both the base vectors and the map details. After generating the terrein these products were combined using Photoshop, and the final map produced in Illustrator

After much debate about the labeling of natural features, it was decided that the only labels included in the map would be of major populated places within the region, countries both inside and outside of the region, and large bodies of water.
